Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 2026

JieLabs ("we," "our," or "us") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the information entrusted to us. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, how we protect it, and the choices available to you.

Our software platforms and underlying systems are designed with user privacy and operational data sovereignty as core architectural principles.

Information We Collect

Depending on how you interact with our website or products, we may collect the following classifications of information:

1. Contact Information

When voluntarily provided through our consultation forms, request channels, or direct email, we collect:

  • Name and primary identification data
  • Business email address and phone details
  • Organization or enterprise entity name
  • Job title or corporate role context

This information is used strictly to respond to specific inquiries, provide system demonstrations, and communicate regarding our technical products and services.

2. Website Information

Like most modern platforms, we may automatically collect limited technical data through standard web server operations, including:

  • Browser type and software versions
  • Device identification and hardware parameters
  • Internet Protocol (IP) address
  • Operating system frameworks
  • Specific pages visited, including date and time of access

This data helps us evaluate platform health, monitor performance, and enforce structural website security.

3. Product & System Information

Customers using hosted or licensed JieLabs software solutions generate operational metrics necessary to ensure functional platform stability, including:

  • User authentication events and access timestamps
  • Local environment configuration settings
  • System health metrics and failure alerts
  • Administrative audit trails
  • Operational telemetry indicators

This data is compiled solely to operate, secure, track anomalies, and maintain our systems.

What We Do Not Do

  • We never sell personal information to brokers or third parties.
  • We never rent customer contact lists or database records.
  • We never share customer information with external marketing or advertising agencies.
  • We never pass corporate or user text arrays to train public AI models.
  • We never access client deployment environments unless explicitly requested and authorized.

How Information Is Used

Information collected within our frameworks may be used to:

  • Provide requested software architectures and consulting deliverables
  • Respond directly to technical or enterprise consultation inquiries
  • Analyze diagnostic telemetry to improve our application pipelines
  • Protect and secure our platforms from cyber threats or malicious use
  • Detect, prevent, and mitigate unauthorized infrastructure activity
  • Fulfill operational baseline legal requirements and regulatory protocols

Data Security

Protecting customer information is a core software design principle at JieLabs. Our platforms employ multiple security layers, including advanced infrastructure encryption, strict Role-Based Access Controls (RBAC), zero-trust authentication loops, immutable system audit logging, secure network credential storage, and private environment isolation boundaries to shield information from exposure.

Data Sovereignty

We build solutions optimized for data sovereignty. Many JieLabs systems are architected to support private, on-premises, or isolated cloud infrastructure deployments where data processing and persistent storage remain entirely within the customer's secure perimeter, keeping total control in your hands.

Healthcare Data & Compliance

JieLabs develops advanced software tools used by healthcare organizations. Our enterprise clients remain solely responsible for determining whether their specific infrastructure implementations are subject to applicable healthcare regulations, including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

Where required, JieLabs will execute appropriate contractual agreements, including formal Business Associate Agreements (BAAs), to fully support regulated enterprise healthcare deployments.

Third-Party Services

Certain platform integrations may connect with external providers selected and managed entirely by the customer, including:

  • Electronic Health Record (EHR) data environments
  • Federated enterprise identity providers (OIDC/SAML)
  • Private cloud hosting networks
  • Automated administrative email delivery channels

Each external system maintains its own isolated privacy policies and handling methods.

Data Retention

We retain personal and technical data records only for the duration required to satisfy active system tasks, maintain necessary operational security, honor corporate contracts, or fulfill relevant legal compliance frameworks. Customers running self-hosted platforms retain exclusive authority over their own database preservation timelines.

Your Rights

Depending on your regional location and applicable local statutes, you may possess specific rights regarding your data, including the right to request comprehensive access to your information, update inaccurate data fields, request programmatic deletion where legally viable, or withdraw consent for data processing where previously granted.

Children's Privacy

Our enterprise platforms, developer portals, and marketing websites are not engineered for, targeted toward, or intended for individuals under the age of 13.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically to adjust for new platform offerings, structural compliance revisions, or shifting regulatory mandates. Any updated draft will be published instantly on this page with a revised effective timestamp.
